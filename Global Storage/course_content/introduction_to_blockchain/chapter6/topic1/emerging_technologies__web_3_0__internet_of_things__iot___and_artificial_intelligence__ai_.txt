    <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold mb-4">Web 3.0: The Decentralized Web</h2>

    <p class="mb-4">Web 3.0 represents a fundamental shift in how we interact with the internet. Moving beyond the centralized platforms of Web 2.0, it embraces decentralization, user empowerment, and enhanced security through technologies like blockchain and semantic web concepts.</p>

    <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">Key Features of Web 3.0:</h3>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li><b>Decentralization:</b> Power and data are distributed across networks, reducing reliance on centralized authorities.</li>
        <li><b>Blockchain Technology:</b> Enables secure and transparent transactions, fostering trust and immutability.</li>
        <li><b>Semantic Web:</b>  Data is given well-defined meaning, enabling machines and humans to understand and process information more effectively.</li>
        <li><b>Artificial Intelligence:</b> AI algorithms enhance user experiences and enable personalized content delivery.</li>
        <li><b>Immersive Experiences:</b> Integration with technologies like AR/VR creates richer and more engaging online interactions. </li>
    </ul>

    <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">Impact of Web 3.0:</h3>
    <p class="mb-4">Web 3.0 promises to revolutionize various sectors:</p>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li><b>Finance:</b> Decentralized finance (DeFi) offers alternative financial systems.</li>
        <li><b>Data Management:</b> Users gain more control over their data and its usage.</li>

        <li><b>Social Networking:</b> Decentralized social platforms prioritize user privacy and ownership.</li>

    <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold mb-4">Internet of Things (IoT): Connecting the Physical and Digital Worlds</h2>

    <p class="mb-4">The IoT refers to a vast network of interconnected physical devices embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies that enable them to collect and exchange data. This interconnectedness creates a smart ecosystem where devices can communicate and automate tasks, improving efficiency and convenience.</p>

    <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">Key Components of IoT:</h3>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li><b>Sensors:</b> Collect data from the environment, such as temperature, pressure, and location.</li>
        <li><b>Connectivity:</b> Enables devices to communicate with each other and the cloud via Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or cellular networks.</li>
        <li><b>Data Processing:</b>  Collected data is analyzed and processed to extract meaningful insights.</li>
        <li><b>Actuators:</b> Physical components that can be controlled remotely, such as switches, motors, and valves.</li>
        <li><b>Cloud Computing:</b> Provides the infrastructure for data storage, processing, and analysis.</li>
    </ul>

    <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">Applications of IoT:</h3>
    <p class="mb-4">IoT is transforming various industries:</p>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li><b>Smart Homes:</b> Automated control of lighting, appliances, and security systems.</li>
        <li><b>Industrial Automation:</b> Real-time monitoring and control of industrial processes, improving efficiency and reducing downtime.</li>
        <li><b>Healthcare:</b> Remote patient monitoring, wearable health trackers, and smart medical devices.</li>
        <li><b>Transportation:</b> Connected cars, traffic management systems, and autonomous vehicles.</li>
        <li><b>Agriculture:</b> Precision farming techniques using sensors to optimize crop yields.</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="mb-4">Artificial Intelligence (AI) involves developing computer systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, and decision-making.  AI encompasses various subfields, including machine learning, deep learning, and natural language processing.</p>

    <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">Types of AI:</h3>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li><b>Narrow or Weak AI:</b> Designed for specific tasks, like image recognition or language translation.</li>
        <li><b>General or Strong AI:</b> Hypothetical AI with human-level intelligence capable of performing any intellectual task that a human being can.</li>
        <li><b>Super AI:</b> Hypothetical AI surpassing human intelligence in all aspects.</li>
    </ul>


    <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">Applications of AI:</h3>
    <p class="mb-4">AI is being applied across a wide range of domains:</p>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li><b>Healthcare:</b> Disease diagnosis, drug discovery, and personalized medicine.</li>
        <li><b>Finance:</b> Fraud detection, algorithmic trading, and risk assessment.</li>
        <li><b>Customer Service:</b> Chatbots and virtual assistants providing automated support.</li>
        <li><b>Robotics:</b>  Developing intelligent robots for manufacturing, healthcare, and other applications.</li>
        <li><b>Autonomous Vehicles:</b> Enabling self-driving cars and other autonomous systems.</li>
    </ul>
