    <h1 class="text-3xl font-bold mb-4">Types of AI Agents</h1>

    <h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">1. Utility-Based Agents</h2>
    <p class="mb-4">These agents aim to maximize a specific utility function, often involving complex calculations and optimization. Examples include:</p>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li>Financial Trading Agents (optimizing portfolio returns)</li>
        <li>Dynamic Pricing Systems (adjusting prices based on demand and market conditions)</li>
        <li>Smart Grid Controllers (managing energy distribution efficiently)</li>
        <li>Personalized Content Recommendation Engines (suggesting relevant content to users)</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">2. Goal-Based Agents</h2>
    <p class="mb-4">These agents strive to achieve specific goals, planning actions and adapting to changing circumstances. Examples include:</p>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li>Roomba (navigating and cleaning a room)</li>
        <li>Project Management Software (scheduling tasks and managing resources)</li>
        <li>Video Game AI (controlling non-player characters)</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">3. Model-Based Reflex Agents</h2>
    <p class="mb-4">These agents maintain an internal model of the world and react to changes in their environment based on predefined rules and the model's state. Examples include:</p>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li>Autonomous Vehicles (navigating roads and reacting to traffic)</li>
        <li>Modern Irrigation Systems (adjusting watering schedules based on soil moisture and weather conditions)</li>
        <li>Home Automation Systems (controlling lighting, temperature, and other appliances based on user preferences and sensor data)</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">4. Learning Agents</h2>
    <p class="mb-4">These agents improve their performance over time by learning from data and experience. Examples include:</p>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li>Fraud Detection Systems (identifying fraudulent transactions)</li>
        <li>Content Recommendation Engines (personalizing recommendations based on user behavior)</li>
        <li>Speech Recognition Software (converting spoken words to text)</li>
        <li>Adaptive Thermostats (learning user preferences and optimizing temperature settings)</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">5. Hierarchical Agents</h2>
    <p class="mb-4">These agents decompose complex tasks into smaller subtasks, managed by different levels of the hierarchy.  Examples include:</p>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li>Manufacturing Robots (performing complex assembly operations)</li>
        <li>Air Traffic Control Systems (managing aircraft movements)</li>
        <li>Autonomous Warehouse Robots (optimizing storage and retrieval of goods)</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">6. Robotic Agents</h2>
    <p class="mb-4">These agents interact with the physical world through sensors and actuators.  Examples include:</p>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li>Assembly Line Robots (performing repetitive tasks in manufacturing)</li>
        <li>Surgical Robots (assisting surgeons with minimally invasive procedures)</li>
        <li>Agricultural Robots (automating tasks like planting, harvesting, and spraying)</li>
        <li>Service Robots (performing tasks in hospitality, healthcare, and other industries)</li>
    </ul>


     <h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">7. Virtual Assistants</h2>
    <p class="mb-4">These agents interact with users through natural language interfaces, providing information and performing tasks. Examples include:</p>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li>Siri (Apple's virtual assistant)</li>
        <li>Alexa (Amazon's virtual assistant)</li>
        <li>Google Assistant (Google's virtual assistant)</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">8. Multi-Agent Systems</h2>
    <p class="mb-4">These systems involve multiple agents interacting and collaborating to achieve a common goal. Examples include:</p>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li>Traffic Management Systems (optimizing traffic flow)</li>
        <li>Smart Grids for Energy Management (balancing energy supply and demand)</li>
        <li>Supply Chain and Logistics (managing the flow of goods and materials)</li>
        <li>Autonomous Swarm Robotics (coordinating a group of robots to perform a task)</li>
    </ul>

     <h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mb-2">9. Simple Reflex Agents</h2>
    <p class="mb-4">These agents react directly to percepts (inputs from their environment) based on predefined rules, without maintaining an internal state or model. Examples include:</p>
    <ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-4">
        <li>Thermostats (turning heating or cooling on/off based on temperature readings)</li>
        <li>Automatic Doors (opening when a sensor detects motion)</li>
        <li>Smoke Detectors (triggering an alarm when smoke is detected)</li>
        <li>Basic Spam Filters (filtering emails based on keywords)</li>
    </ul>
